Maintenance Requirements for Fleet Warranties (New York Edition)

New York state laws require that motor vehicles be properly maintained in order to keep their warranty valid. If the vehicle's warranty is voided due to lack of proper maintenance, it may not be covered under the fleet warranty. Therefore, it is essential for fleet operators to adhere to the following maintenance requirements.

Regular Maintenance Schedule

  • Tire Pressure and Condition: Check tire pressure regularly and maintain the recommended levels as outlined in the owner's manual. Also, ensure that tires are properly inflated to avoid tire failure.
  • Oil and Fluids: Regularly check and replace engine oil, transmission fluid, and coolant as recommended by the manufacturer.
  • Brake System: Check and replace brake pads, rotors, and drums as necessary to prevent brake failure.
  • Air Filter: Replace the air filter every 15,000 to 30,000 miles to maintain engine performance and prevent damage.
  • Battery: Check and replace the battery as necessary to prevent starting problems and electrical system failures.

State-Specific Requirements

New York state laws have additional requirements for vehicle maintenance. These include:

  • Safety Inspection: A safety inspection must be performed on the vehicle every 12 months or 12,000 miles, whichever comes first.
  • Smoke and Emissions Tests: The vehicle must pass a smoke and emissions test every 12 months or 50,000 miles, whichever comes first.
  • Snow Tires: Vehicles must be equipped with snow tires during the winter months (December to April) or be equipped with four-wheel drive.
  • Headlamp Beams: Vehicles must be equipped with headlamps that are approved by the New York State Department of Motor Vehicles.

Fleet Warranty Coverage

Fleet warranty coverage may vary depending on the manufacturer and type of warranty. It is essential to review the warranty terms and conditions to understand what is covered and what is not. Additionally, fleet operators must ensure that all maintenance records are up-to-date and available for inspection by the manufacturer or warranty provider.

Consequences of Non-Compliance

Failure to comply with New York state laws and manufacturer requirements may result in voiding the fleet warranty. This can lead to costly repairs and maintenance for fleet operators. It is essential to establish a maintenance schedule and follow it consistently to ensure that the warranty remains valid.

Best Practices

  • Establish a maintenance schedule and follow it consistently.
  • Keep accurate and up-to-date maintenance records.
  • Use genuine manufacturer-approved parts and materials.
  • Inspect vehicles regularly and address any issues promptly.
  • Routine maintenance should include regular checks on: Tire pressure and condition, Oil and fluids, Brake system, Air filter, and Battery
